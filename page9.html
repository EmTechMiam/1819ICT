Cyber Threats

Hacking

An act committed by an intruder by accessing your computer without your permission. Hackers (the person doing hacking) are basically computer programmers who have advanced understanding of computers and misuses this knowledgefor devious reasons.

(Image Source: indianatoday.in)

Phishing

A technique of extracting confidential information such as credit card numbers and username password combos by masquerading as a legitimate enterprise. 
It is typically carried out by spoofing. It is usually done through sending an email containing links that appears to be a legitimate website yet it is suspicious.

(Image Source: Malwarebytes.lab)

Identity Theft

Identity theft occurs when a criminal gains access to your personal information (such as your name, address, date of birth or bank account details) to steal money or gain other benefits.  The information collected may be used in other crimes like stealing to hide their real identity.

(Image Source: epaisa.com)

Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is bullying that takes place using electronic technology such as cell phones, computers, and tablets as well as communication tools including social media sites, text messages, chat, and websites.

(Image Source: pshychologicalscience.org)

Illegal Content

Prohibited offensive online content is content that would be classified X18+ or RC (refused classification) by the Classification Board. Material classified R18+ is prohibited if it is not subject to a Restricted Access System, while content classified MA15+ may be prohibited in certain circumstances.

Examples are sexual abuse material or child abuse exploitation or either terrorist-related content.

(Image Source: coe.int)

Online Fraud & Scams 

Online offers various platforms of services that are too good to be true. These are thechances where criminals take the advantage to commit frauds and scams. 

If you received any SMS which is suspicious as a scam, don't open or otherwise delete it.
